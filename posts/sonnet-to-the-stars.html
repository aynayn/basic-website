Oh, gilded dust upon the velvet night,
A million sorrows and a billion dreams.
You flicker, holding every memory's light,
And shimmer brightly in the moonlit streams.
The lonely wanderer who looks on you,
Finds comfort in your cold and distant grace,
A promise that is ancient, deep, and true,
To shine forever from your silent space.
So let me be a star, though I am small,
A tiny light to guide a weary soul,
To rise above the shadows, stand up tall,
And make a fractured spirit once again whole.

For in your gaze, I find a kind of peace,
A solace that will never ever cease.
